History & Culture

Cape Town has a complex and fascinating history shaped by Dutch, British, and indigenous influences. The city was first established by Dutch settlers in 1652 as a supply station for the Dutch East India Company. Over the centuries, it developed into a melting pot of cultures and traditions, evident in its architecture, languages, and customs.

Cape Town played a significant role during the apartheid era and was the site of major resistance movements. Today, it's a city that embraces its past while looking toward a more inclusive future. From the historic District Six Museum to Robben Island – where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned – visitors can explore the depth of the city's cultural heritage.

"Cape Town’s diversity is its strength – a city where every street tells a different story."

Must-See Attractions

One cannot visit Cape Town without taking the cable car up **Table Mountain**, one of the New Seven Natural Wonders of the World. From the summit, the panoramic views of the city, ocean, and surrounding mountains are truly breathtaking. Adventure seekers can also hike up via Platteklip Gorge for a more active experience.

The **Victoria & Alfred Waterfront** is a bustling hub of restaurants, shops, museums, and boat tours. Just a short drive away, you’ll find the **Cape Winelands**, where historic estates and lush vineyards provide the perfect escape. The **Cape of Good Hope**, **Chapman's Peak Drive**, and **Boulders Beach (home to African penguins)** are all essential parts of the Cape Town experience.
